Overview

The Master’s Degree in Applied Bioinformatics represents a collaborative effort between the Schools of Biology of the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki and of Science and Technology of the International Hellenic University.

Students will receive theoretical and practical cutting-edge interdisciplinary postgraduate level education in high-throughput data acquisition, big data handling, analysis, and interpretation of biological information that can be used in several facets of biology that include molecular and evolutionary biology, ecological science, biomedicine and drug discovery, metagenomics, and others. Students will acquire advanced coding skills and will be trained to modern biological and mathematical concepts to develop pipelines and analyze a wide range of data that range from the molecular level (genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, epigenomics etc.), to the functional level (molecular pathway analysis, drug target discovery etc.), to the ecosystem level (metagenomics, ecological modelling, precision agriculture etc.), and others. Students will also have the opportunity to test, refine, and further develop their knowledge through capstone projects, and a Master-level thesis under the supervision of one of the several top-level instructors that will teach at the programme.

The programme is designed to educate the next generation of biologists and data scientists to transforming their respective sectors. Several full and partial scholarships will be available.

The first intake of students is for 2022-23.

Prospective students are encouraged to contact: ikappas@bio.auth.gr (Assoc. Prof. Ilias Kappas) or spiros.papakostas@gmail.com (Assoc. Prof. Spiros Papakostas).
